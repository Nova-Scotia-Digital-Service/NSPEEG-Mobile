Sudden Collapse (Cardiac Arrest)

  1. Call for help! Ask someone to notify the Floor Emergency Warden, get First Aid Kit and AED (Automated External Defibrillator) immediately.

  2. Complete a scene survey and make the area safe.

  3. Call 911 or 9-911, if someone else is nearby have them do it for you. Have caller follow dispatcher's instructions.

  4. Assess Airway, Breathing, Circulation (ABCs) ‑ immediately start CPR if necessary and utilize an AED

  5. If possible have someone meet the ambulance at the building address and bring them to you.

  6. Continue CPR until directed by First Responders (note - sometimes First Responders may wish assistance with patient care).

  7. Complete your departmental Incident Report and inform your manager.

Serious Injury or Illness

  1. Remain calm and call out for help. Call or have someone call 911 or 9-911.

  2. Complete a scene survey and make the area safe.

  3. Stay with the injured or ill person and await emergency services.

  4. Provide first aid for the injuries.

  5. Do not attempt to move injured person, unless they are in imminent danger. Do not transport in personal vehicle unless directed by paramedics.

  6. Stay with the injured person until First Responders arrive.

  7. Complete a departmental Incident Report and inform your Manager.

Injury or Illness

  1. Remain calm. Ask for consent from the individual to provide first aid.

  2. Locate a Floor Emergency Warden or first aider.

  3. When in doubt, call 911 or 9-911.

  4. Provide first aid for the injuries.

  5. If medical attention is required but no ambulance necessary, take the individual to the hospital or clinic by taxi. Or wait with them while a loved one picks them up. Do NOT use personal vehicles.

  6. Do not send injured person alone and insist that they do not drive themselves to seek treatment.

  7. Inform your Manager and complete a departmental Incident Report.
