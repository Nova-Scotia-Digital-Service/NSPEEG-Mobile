Bomb threats can be received in a variety of ways. Most bomb threats are made by persons who want to create an atmosphere of general anxiety or panic. All such threats must be taken seriously and handled as though a bomb is in the building.

Bomb Threat by phone

  1. Remain Calm. Listen to the caller, be calm and courteous. Do not interrupt the caller. Try to keep the caller on the line.

  2. Without upsetting caller, try to obtain as much information as possible. Ask questions, such as "Where is the bomb?", "When is it going to go off?", "What kind is it?", "What does it look like?"

  3. Take notes on what is said and your observations about background noise, voice, language, etc.

  4. Notify someone in the office while the call is still ongoing. They should contact 911 or 9 ‑ 911.

  5. Initiate call trace action (if available) while the call is ongoing (*57).

  6. Follow any instructions given by your Supervisor, Security, or First Responders.

  7. At the direction of 911, pull the fire alarm.

Suspicious Object or Package

If you find or receive a suspicious object, package, or mail:

  1. Remain Calm. Do not touch or move the object.

  2. If you are holding the object gently set it down on a solid surface or on the floor.

  3. Do not cover it. Do not attempt to disarm it.

  4. Close doors to reduce air flow, and isolate the area.

  5. Alert your supervisor and Security if available.

  6. Alert others, leave the building.

  7. Call 911 or 9-911 from government phones when safe to do so

  8. If you come in contact with package contents,

    1. Remain where you are and close the doors.

    2. Await further instructions from First Responders.

Otherwise, leave the immediate area and remain out of the area until told you can return.
